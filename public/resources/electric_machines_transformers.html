1 Ampere's Law & Magneto Motive Force (MMF)

Ampere's Law states that where there is an electric current, there will be an associated magnetic field. The closed line integral of magnetic field intensity H equals the total current enclosed by that path:

∫ H · dl = Σ Currents Enclosed = N · I

The product NI is known as the Magneto Motive Force (MMF), which acts as the excitation parameter of a magnetic circuit. Its SI unit is the Ampere-Turn (A-T).

2 Magnetic Flux Density & Permeability

When a magnetic field intensity H is established inside a core material, it amplifies the magnetic field to establish Magnetic Flux Density (B), measured in Tesla (T):

B = μ · H

Where μ = μ₀μᵣ.
μ₀ (Permeability of free space) = 4π × 10⁻⁷ H/m.
μᵣ is the relative permeability of our iron core.

Total Flux Calculation:

Flux is given by the product of flux density (B) and cross-sectional area (A):

φ = B · A  (Weber, Wb)

4 Air Gaps Integration

When an air gap of length g is introduced into the iron core path length l, it introduces a very high reluctance in series with that of iron:

φ = MMF / (R_iron + R_air)

R_iron = l / (μ₀ μᵣ A),     R_air = g / (μ₀ A)

5 AC Core Loss Formulation

Under AC excitation, real power is lost inside the magnetic core. This core loss is comprised of two distinct physical phenomena:

1. Hysteresis Loss

Caused by molecular friction during reversals of magnetic domains. The power loss is given by Steinmetz formulation:

P_h = k_h · f · B_m^n

where 'n' (Steinmetz exponent) typically ranges from 1.5 to 2.5.

2. Eddy Current Loss

Caused by circulating electric loops induced inside the conducting magnet core. Proportional to frequency squared:

P_e = k_e · f² · B_m²

Low reluctance/powdered cores and thin structural laminations keep Eddy loop losses minimal.

6 Equivalent Circuit of a Transformer

The transformer equivalent circuit models electrical winding parameters on both sides, combined with parallel magnetizing shunt branch components (Rc and Lm):

R₁ & X₁ (Primary) Primary copper windings series resistance and leakage reactance.
R₂' & X₂' (Reflected Secondary) Secondary winding series resistance and leakage reactance, scaled by squared turns ratios: (N1/N2)².
Shunt Branch (Rc & Lm) No-load parameters. Rc is core loss resistance equivalent. Lm is core magnetizing inductance.
